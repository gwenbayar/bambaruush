# Bambaruush — Opening Scene
### "The First Star" · the night Aav shows Od the sky

*This is the emotional cornerstone of the app: the moment that explains why every word becomes a star, and why the smallest light matters. It is written in two parts — a prose version to set the voice and tone for the whole team, and a playable first-lesson script that turns the same moment into game beats. Both teach the same first word: **од** (star).*

> **A note on the Mongolian:** transliterations and spellings below are working drafts. Have a native speaker confirm every word, the child-voiced audio, and the feel of the names before anything ships.

---

## Part 1 — The scene (prose, for tone)

The sun had slipped behind the far hills, and the whole steppe had gone the deep blue of evening. Inside the ger the fire was low and orange. Outside, the grass had stopped moving. Everything was getting ready to be quiet.

Aav was leaving in the morning. He would be gone a long time, out with the herd, far across the land where the grass grew greener. His bags were packed by the door.

Od wanted to help. Od *always* wanted to help. But the saddle was too heavy, and the rope was too long, and the camel was much, much too tall.

"You're too little, little one," said Sar, not unkindly, as she hurried past with a bridle. "Maybe next year."

So Od went and sat in the doorway of the ger, where the warm air met the cool air, and looked at their own paws, and felt as small as a child can feel.

That is where Aav found them.

He did not say *don't be sad*. He just sat down in the doorway too, his big shoulder warm against Od's small one, and looked out at the dark with them for a while. Then he lifted one paw and pointed straight up.

"There," he said. "The very first one. Do you see it?"

High up in the blue-going-black, one small light had come out. Just one. Faint. Easy to miss.

"That," said Aav, "is an **od**. A star." He said the word slowly, the way you hand someone something precious. "*Od.*"

"Od," whispered Od. "Like me."

"Like you," said Aav, and Od could hear him smiling. "We named you for it."

Od looked at the little star. It was so small. All around it the sky was getting crowded with bigger, brighter ones. "But it's the *littlest*," Od said. "Nobody would even see it."

Aav was quiet for a moment. Then he pointed again, to a star low over the hills, steady and calm.

"You see that one? That star never moves. All the others wander across the sky all night long — but not that one. It stays. We call it **Altan Gadas**, the golden stake, because the whole sky is tied to it like a horse to a post." He looked down at Od. "When I am far away in the dark, and I cannot see the road, and I do not know which way is home — I find that little star. And it shows me the way back to you."

Od stared at it. "A *little* star does that?"

"The truest one does," said Aav. "Not the brightest, little one. The truest."

The fire popped softly behind them. Somewhere out in the dark, Bars the big dog sighed and rolled over.

"Here is what we will do," Aav said. "While I am away, you will learn the words for things. The name of every animal, every color, every place. And each word you truly learn —" he reached up and pinched the little star between two claws, gently, as if he could lift it right out of the sky "— I will hang up *here*. As a star."

"In the *sky?*"

"In our sky. So that every night, no matter how far I have gone, I can look up and count them. And I will know exactly how much my little one has grown." He set the imaginary star back in its place. "And one day there will be so many that you will be the one lighting the way. You will be how this whole family finds its way home."

Od looked up at the one small star, and the one small star looked back, and it did not seem quite so little anymore.

"Can I learn one now?" said Od. "Before you go?"

Aav laughed — a low, warm sound, the best sound. "You already did," he said. "*Od.* Star. That's the first one." He touched Od's nose. "Look up. There it is."

And Od looked up, and there it was: the very first star, shining its own small way.

## Part 2 — The first lesson (playable script)

*Game type:* **Listen & find the picture** (the gentlest frame — see Story Guide §6).
*Teaches:* the word **од** / *star*.
*Goal of this lesson:* the player hears the word, finds the star, and earns the very first permanent sky-star — establishing the core promise of the whole app in under two minutes.

### Beat 0 — Cold open (cinematic, ~20s, skippable)

| Element | Content |
|---|---|
| **Visuals** | The ger doorway at dusk, deep blue. Aav and Od sit shoulder to shoulder, small against the big sky. One faint star fades in, high up. |
| **Audio (Aav, warm, unhurried)** | "There. The very first one. Do you see it?" |
| **Mood** | Quiet, cozy. No music sting, no UI yet. Just the two of them and the dark. |

### Beat 1 — Introduce the word (audio + picture together)

> **Rule:** never test a word the child has not first *heard and seen*. (Story Guide §8)

| Element | Content |
|---|---|
| **Visuals** | The single star gently pulses. A soft label appears beneath it: **од**. |
| **Audio (Aav)** | "This is an *od*. A star. … *Od.*" (the word said twice, slowly) |
| **Player action** | None yet — the child just listens. A glowing **tap-to-hear-again** speaker icon sits by the word. |

### Beat 2 — The ask (the help-framing)

| Element | Content |
|---|---|
| **Audio (Aav)** | "Can you find the *od* for me, little one?" |
| **Visuals** | Three big, friendly pictures slide up from the bottom: a **star**, a **flower**, a **boot** (clearly different shapes, easy for a 4–5 year old). The word **од** stays on screen with its speaker icon. |
| **Framing** | The child is *helping Aav*, not taking a test. |

### Beat 3 — The tap (with the kindness rules)

> **Rules:** no failure states; wrong answers are "let's try again," never a buzzer or a loss. No punishing timer. (Story Guide §8)

| If the child taps… | Response |
|---|---|
| **The star (correct)** | The star picture lifts, sparkles, and flies *up into the sky* to take its place. Aav: "That's it! That's the *od*." Warm chime. |
| **A wrong picture** | The picture wiggles gently and settles back. Soft, encouraging tone — Aav: "Hmm, that's the boot. Listen again —" and the word **од** replays on its own. No red X, no sound of failure. The child simply tries again. As many times as they need. |
| **Nothing for a while** | The correct picture gives a small, friendly glow-pulse to invite the tap. The bears wait patiently. |

### Beat 4 — The first star is placed (the payoff)

*This is the most important fifteen seconds in the app. It establishes the entire reward promise.*

| Element | Content |
|---|---|
| **Visuals** | The star the child found settles into the dark sky and begins to *shine on its own.* The camera drifts up so the child sees the whole empty night above it — room for hundreds more. |
| **Audio (Aav)** | "You learned your very first word. So I'm hanging it up here — as a star. Our first one." |
| **System** | First permanent sky-star is awarded and saved. A tiny, quiet "★ 1" appears — Od's sky has begun. (This star can never be spent; see Story Guide §7, Track B.) |
| **Emotional beat** | Aav: "Every word you learn, I'll hang up here. So wherever I am, I'll always know how much you've grown." |

### Beat 5 — Bars celebrates + first currency (teaching the other track)

| Element | Content |
|---|---|
| **Visuals** | Bars the big dog bounds in, does his happy spin, and barks once. A small handful of **Odon** drops with a cheerful sound. |
| **Audio** | No words — just Bars's bark and a warm jingle. |
| **Purpose** | The child meets the second reward in its simplest form: *you did a good thing, here's a little something to spend.* (Bars's always-available snack loop is introduced right after, as the gentlest possible first purchase — Story Guide §7.) |

### Beat 6 — Soft close (the hook to return)

| Element | Content |
|---|---|
| **Visuals** | Back to the cozy doorway. One star now glows in the sky above the ger. Aav stands, shoulders his bag for the morning. |
| **Audio (Aav)** | "Learn me some more while I'm away, little one. I'll be counting them." |
| **Player choice** | Two gentle buttons: **"Learn another word"** (next lesson) and **"All done for now"** (home). No pressure, no streak-shaming. |

## Why this scene carries the whole app

- **The first word *is* the hero's name *is* the currency of growth.** "Od / star" teaches a word, names the protagonist, and creates the first sky-star — three things at once, so the child feels the core loop before any rule is explained.
- **The promise is the engine.** "Every word becomes a star, and the stars are how I find my way home" turns every future lesson into an act of love toward the away-father — the bond from Story Guide §4.
- **The theme is shown, not told.** Od is called "too little to help," then learns that the *littlest, truest* star is the one others navigate by. Nobody states the lesson; the sky does. ("Every light shines its own way.")
- **The kindness rules are visible from second one.** No failure, no buzzer, patient bears, warm praise for the specific thing done. The child's very first experience of the app is safety.
